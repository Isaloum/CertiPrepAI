📋 AWS Exam Format & Logistics

What is the format of most AWS certification exams? ▼

Most AWS certification exams consist of 65 questions (multiple-choice and multiple-response) completed in 130 minutes. Specialty exams may vary slightly. All exams are scored on a scale of 100–1000, with a passing score of 720 for most certifications.

How much do AWS exams cost? ▼

Foundation exams cost $100 USD. Associate exams cost $150 USD. Professional and Specialty exams cost $300 USD. Practice exams available through AWS are $40 USD each. Prices may vary by region.

How long are AWS certifications valid? ▼

All AWS certifications are valid for 3 years. You can recertify by retaking the same exam, passing a higher-level exam, or completing approved recertification activities. AWS sends reminders 6 months before expiration.

What happens if I fail an exam? ▼

You must wait 14 days before retaking. There's no limit on attempts, but you pay the full fee each time. Use your score report to identify weak domains and focus your study there before retaking.

☁️ Key AWS Concepts

What is the difference between Security Groups and NACLs? ▼

Security Groups: Stateful, instance-level firewall. Supports allow rules only. Return traffic is automatically allowed.

NACLs: Stateless, subnet-level firewall. Supports both allow and deny rules. Must explicitly allow both inbound and outbound traffic including ephemeral ports.

What is the AWS shared responsibility model? ▼

AWS Responsibility (Security OF the cloud): Physical infrastructure, hardware, networking, facilities.

Customer Responsibility (Security IN the cloud): Data encryption, IAM, network configuration, application security, guest OS patching.

What is the AWS Well-Architected Framework? ▼

The Well-Architected Framework has 6 pillars: Operational Excellence, Security, Reliability, Performance Efficiency, Cost Optimization, and Sustainability. These pillars appear heavily across all Associate and Professional-level exams.

💡 Exam Strategy

What are some exam day tips? ▼

• Read each question carefully — they often contain key requirements
• Eliminate obviously wrong answers first
• Budget ~2 minutes per question (130 min ÷ 65 questions)
• Flag difficult questions and return to them later
• Look for keywords like "most cost-effective," "least operational overhead," "highly available"
• When uncertain, choose managed AWS services over self-managed solutions

How can I tell if I'm exam-ready? ▼

You're likely ready when you:
• Consistently score 75%+ on all 4 mock exams
• Can explain the "why" behind answers, not just memorize them
• Understand trade-offs between different architectural choices
• Are comfortable with all exam domains
• Can design solutions considering cost, performance, and security simultaneously

🚀 Career Path

What's the recommended certification path? ▼

Recommended paths:
• Beginner: CLF-C02 → SAA-C03
• Developer track: DVA-C02 → DOP-C02
• Data/ML track: DEA-C01 → MLA-C01 → AIF-C01
• Security track: SAA-C03 → SCS-C03
• Networking track: SAA-C03 → ANS-C01
• Top of ladder: SAP-C02 or DOP-C02 (Professional level)

How do I maintain my certification? ▼

To recertify before your 3-year expiration:
• Retake the same-level exam
• Pass a higher-level exam (Professional or Specialty automatically renews lower certs)
• Complete AWS recertification training when available

AWS sends reminders 6 months before expiration. Plan your recertification early!
